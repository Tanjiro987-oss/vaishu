My dearest,

A Letter of Sorry

Some apologies are bigger than words — but words are all I have left

💔
May 9, 2026  ·  Written with a heavy heart

My dearest,

I have started this letter a hundred times and deleted it a hundred more — because no arrangement of words feels worthy enough of what I owe you. But today, on your birthday, I refuse to let silence speak for me any longer.

I am sorry.

I am sorry for the moments I made you feel small when you were the biggest thing in my world. I am sorry for the times I chose pride over peace, distance over closeness, and silence over the conversations we needed to have.

I am sorry for February 14th — a day that was once our beginning and became our ending. Of all the days the universe could have chosen, it chose the one that was supposed to be about love. I have sat with that irony every night since.

You deserved someone who showed up fully — not just in the beautiful moments, but in the difficult ones too. You deserved consistency, gentleness, and the kind of love that never makes you question its presence. I fell short of that. I know it.

This is not a plea for anything in return. You owe me nothing — not a reply, not forgiveness, not even a second thought. This letter exists only because you deserve to know that someone in this world carries the weight of every hurt they ever caused you, and wishes more than anything they could undo it.

I hope this birthday brings you everything I failed to —
warmth that never wavers, love that never conditions, and a joy
so deep it reaches the parts of you that were ever made to feel less than whole.

You are nineteen and radiant, and you deserve a life that knows it.

Forever sorry, forever yours in memory 🌸
